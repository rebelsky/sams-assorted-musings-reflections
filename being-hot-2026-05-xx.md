Once in a while, when I'm talking to a particularly attractive person, I find myself wanting to ask them a somewhat inappropriate question, which I'll phrase as "_What's it like to be hot?_" I suppose it could be "What's it like to be attractive?"

I'm not sure why I want to know, other than that it's something I wouldn't know about. (That's okay, I'm happy with who I am.) And it's probably a question I'll never ask, because, um, I'm not sure that it can be taken well.

I wonder, in part, because there are aspects that appear to be positives, at least at first glance: People are more inclined to interact with you, people devote more attention to you (which can help you succeed), and, at least in some cases, people tend to rate you higher. For that last issue, I know attractiveness is one of the positive impacts on end-of-course ratings. I also wonder because being attractive seems to carry some power over others.

At the same time, I see that there can be many associated negatives. For women, in particular, there's likely the constant worry about the kinds of assault that are often perpetuated on attractive people. But there's also the question that I raise when we discuss bias in CSC-151: Do people only listen to you / talk to you / partner with you because you are attractive?

I suppose I face a variant of that last question: Do people only listen to or talk to me because I'm a large, old, powerful (in terms of things like being able to assign grades), white-presenting male? I hope not. I assume that what I say is interesting or useful. Or is it that, as a large, old, powerful, white-prsenting male, I just assume that what I say has value?

But that's not the question at play here. I'm still left wondering

> What's it like to be hot?

Don't worry, there's no need to answer the question.
