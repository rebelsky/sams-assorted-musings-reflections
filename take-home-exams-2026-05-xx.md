I recently wrote about [academic honesty and large language models (LLMs)](academic-honesty-llms-2026-05-xxxx). As I noted in that piece, I've seen a significant uptick in the use of LLMs in my CS courses; I'm pretty sure most CS faculty have had similar experiences. Perhaps all faculty have.

My experiences with student use of LLMs makes me question my assessment practices. Historically, I've been a fan of take-home exams because I have found that take-home exams are better for students. Among other things, most students find take-home exams less stressful, students can more readily make use of appropriate resources (which I intend), students can do their work in a less distracting environment, and, for classes that include computer programming, students can rely on the development environment they are used to. I've always said to myself, "While it's possible that some students will cheat, I'd rather not penalize the vast majority of students for the actions of a few."  That's getting harder to say.
