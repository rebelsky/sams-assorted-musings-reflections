A friend says that it's not that the grief shrinks, it's that we grow. However, there are times that the grief temporarily expands enough that it completely envelops me. Strangely enough, the most common time is when I park the car. I'm never completely sure why. Perhaps it's that I drive her car, park in her spot, and park the way she preferred (facing out). I don't know. All I know is that I sit in the front seat, think about her, and sob.

One of my goals fo rthis year was to figure out how I'd manage to live without her. I'm not sure that I've come up with a completely satisfactory answer. However, for the time being, I have one. I'll make it one day at a time.

I so wanted to grow old together. Perhaps we did. I certainly feel old. But she wasn't.
